\chapter*{Abstract}
\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{Abstract}

This book chronicles the evolution of artificial intelligence from its biological and mathematical origins in the 1940s to the transformer-based large language models of the mid-2020s. 

The narrative proceeds chronologically across three volumes:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Foundations (1943–1986):} From the McCulloch--Pitts neuron through Hebbian learning, the perceptron, the first AI winter, and the backpropagation revolution.
    
    \item \textbf{Deep Learning (1986–2017):} The rise of convolutional networks, recurrent networks, long short-term memory, and the ImageNet breakthrough.
    
    \item \textbf{Transformers (2017–2026):} The attention mechanism, GPT and BERT, scaling laws, emergent abilities, and the shift toward agentic systems with tools and governance.
\end{enumerate}

Every major architecture is accompanied by its original mathematical formulation, a complete NumPy scratch implementation, and a critical analysis of its historical significance and modern relevance. This work serves as both a historical record and a technical reference for researchers, students, and practitioners.
