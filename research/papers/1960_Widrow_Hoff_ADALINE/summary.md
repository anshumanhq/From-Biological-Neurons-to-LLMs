# Summary: Adaptive Switching Circuits

**Authors:** Bernard Widrow, Marcian E. Hoff  
**Year:** 1960

Widrow and Hoff introduced the ADALINE (Adaptive Linear Neuron) and the Least Mean Squares (LMS) algorithm—a form of stochastic gradient descent. Unlike Rosenblatt's Perceptron, which used binary error correction, the LMS rule uses a continuous error signal to minimise the Mean Squared Error. This transformed neural network learning into a mathematically grounded optimization problem.
