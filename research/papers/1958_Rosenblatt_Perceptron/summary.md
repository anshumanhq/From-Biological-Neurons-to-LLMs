# Summary: The Perceptron

**Author:** Frank Rosenblatt  
**Year:** 1958  
**DOI:** 10.1037/h0042519

Rosenblatt introduced the Perceptron, the first trainable neural network. It uses a supervised error-correction rule to update weights, and the paper proves that if the data are linearly separable, the algorithm will converge to a solution. The Perceptron demonstrated that machines could learn from data, sparking a wave of research and public interest in neural networks.
