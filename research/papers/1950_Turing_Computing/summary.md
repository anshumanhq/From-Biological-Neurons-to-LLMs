# Summary: Computing Machinery and Intelligence

**Author:** Alan M. Turing  
**Year:** 1950  
**DOI:** 10.1093/mind/LIX.236.433

Turing proposed the Imitation Game (Turing Test) as an operational definition of machine intelligence. He argued that the question "Can machines think?" is too vague and should be replaced with a behavioural test. The paper also introduces the concept of a "child machine"—a machine that learns through education rather than explicit programming—foreshadowing modern machine learning.
