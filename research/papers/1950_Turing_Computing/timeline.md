# Timeline Context: Turing (1950)

- **Preceded by:**
  - 1936: Turing publishes "On Computable Numbers" (UTM).
  - 1943: McCulloch & Pitts formalize the neuron.
  - 1949: Hebb publishes the synaptic learning rule.

- **Contemporaries:**
  - 1950: Claude Shannon publishes "Programming a Computer for Playing Chess."
  - 1950: Norbert Wiener's *The Human Use of Human Beings* (Cybernetics refined).

- **Followed by:**
  - 1956: Dartmouth Conference (AI officially named).
  - 1958: Rosenblatt's Perceptron.
  - 1969: Minsky & Papert's *Perceptrons*.
  - 1980: Searle's Chinese Room (direct response to the Imitation Game).

- **Historical Context Note:** Turing wrote this paper while working on the design of the Automatic Computing Engine (ACE) at the National Physical Laboratory. The paper is essentially a philosophical manifesto for what a general-purpose computer could become.
