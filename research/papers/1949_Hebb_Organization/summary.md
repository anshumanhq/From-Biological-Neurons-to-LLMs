# Summary: The Organization of Behavior

**Author:** Donald O. Hebb  
**Year:** 1949  
**ISBN:** 978-0-8058-4300-0

Hebb proposed the first physiological learning rule: "cells that fire together, wire together." The book introduces the concepts of cell assemblies and phase sequences—distributed, reverberating circuits that sustain activity and form the basis of memory and thought. This work bridged psychology and neurophysiology, establishing the foundation for connectionist learning theories.
