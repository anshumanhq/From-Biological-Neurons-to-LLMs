# Summary: A Logical Calculus of the Ideas Immanent in Nervous Activity

**Authors:** Warren S. McCulloch, Walter H. Pitts  
**Year:** 1943  
**DOI:** 10.1007/BF02478259

The McCulloch-Pitts neuron is the first formal mathematical model of a biological neuron. It treats the neuron as a binary threshold logic unit that can compute propositional logic functions (AND, OR, NOT). The paper proves that any finite logical expression can be realized by a network of such neurons, and conversely that any such network can be reduced to a logical expression. This established neural computation as a rigorous mathematical discipline and laid the foundation for all subsequent neural network research.
